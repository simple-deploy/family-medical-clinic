What Is an ECG?

An ECG (Electrocardiogram) is a medical test used to measure the electrical activity generated by the heart as it contracts. The cardiograph machine displays this data as a trace on a screen or paper, allowing medical practitioners to identify abnormalities and monitor heart function.

ECG testing may be recommended for patients who have a family history of heart disease or conditions that increase cardiac risk.

ECG May Be Recommended For

  • ✔ A family history of heart disease
  • ✔ Smoking
  • ✔ Being overweight
  • ✔ High cholesterol or high blood pressure
  • ✔ Diagnosed diabetes

Conditions ECG Can Help Diagnose

  • ✔ Enlargement of the heart
  • ✔ Congenital heart defects
  • ✔ Abnormal heart rhythms (arrhythmias)
  • ✔ Blocked or damaged arteries
  • ✔ Poor blood supply to the heart
  • ✔ Heart inflammation (pericarditis or myocarditis)
  • ✔ Electrical conduction disturbances
  • ✔ Cardiac complications during emergency monitoring
